Countries Visited




BALI, INDONESIA

In Bali, I had many wonderful experiences. One of them being climbing a volcano named Mount Batur(see picture to the left). A driver picked me and my husband up at our hotel at 2AM in the morning! It was a 2 hour drive to the volcano. Once we got there, people were separated into groups of 4 with each tour guide. It took about 2 hours to climb the volcano. At first, it was easy. However, the higher we went up the steeper the climb. We witnessed the sunrise at the top of the volcano. Luckily, the volcano has been dormant since about the year 2000. Crazy part of this story? I climbed the volcano in a skirt!




MECCA, SAUDI ARABIA

In Mecca, I had a spiritual experience. Every year, there is an auspicious time for Muslims to complete a pilgrimage in Mecca. Muslims follow the religion of Islam and have 5 pillars to follow: Belief, Prayer, Fasting, Zakat(Charity) and Hajj(Pilgrimage). All but the Hajj(Pilgrimage), is required of every Muslim. If a Muslim person has the financial means to complete the pilgrimage, then that pillar is obligatory for them. I had the privelege of completing the pilgrimage last year in August of 2018. Every year the date changes due to the Muslims following the lunar calendar. The date changes about 10 days back per year. The pilgrimage includes many rituals, such as circumambulating the Kaaba (see picture to left) 7 times, walking back and forth 7 times between mountains Safa and Marwa, and many more.




INDIA

I went to India when I was about 6 years old. My mother is from India. My mother's family is not from any well known city. I do not know the English translation of my mother's family's village name. She is from Mazorbari - which translates to "middle village." But there are many middle villages in India, so I am pretty sure that is not the official name for the village. My mother has 7 siblings - 4 brothers and 3 sisters. One of my uncle's family and one of my aunt's family still live in India. The remaining siblings are here in the US. The part of India where my mom is from does not keep birth records. My mother nor any of her siblings know their actual birth date!




Bangladesh

I have been to Bangladesh 3 times. My father is from Bangladesh. He has 4 brothers and 5 sisters, for a grand total of 9 siblings. The last time I went to Bangladesh is when I was about 9 years old. My most vivid memory is when I rode a motorcycle with my uncle. Since a motorcycle can fit only two people max, my uncle and I drove the motorcycle to my aunt's house. I picked some white peppers from my aunt's garden and brought it back to my mom, since I knew she loved peppers. My favorite food to eat was a spicy chickpea dish served in a cup only on the train!
